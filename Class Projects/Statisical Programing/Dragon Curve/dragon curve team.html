Let P be a matrix containing all coordinate pairs for a n-1th iteration of the dragon curve.

$$ P = \begin{bmatrix} (x_1,y_1)\\ (x_2,y_2)\\ (...)\\ (x_k,y_k) \end{bmatrix} $$

To find the coordinate pairs for the nth iteration of the dragon curve, rotate all coordinate pairs in P 90 degrees counterclockwise about the last point in P,$(x_k,y_k)$, and attach them to the end of P in revsere order. i.e. the rotated version of $(x_1,y_1)$ will be attach to P last

To rotate a point around $(x_k,y_k)$, subtract $(x_k,y_k)$ from the point and rotate the point about the origin and add $(x_k,y_k)$ back.

e.g To rotate 90 degrees counterclockwise $(x_1,y_1)$ about $(x_k,y_k)$.

$$ (x_1,y_1) - (x_k,y_k) = (x_1-x_k,y_1-y_k) $$

Then rotate $(x_1-x_k,y_1-y_k)$ 90 degrees counterclockwise about the origin.

$$ (x_1-x_k,y_1-y_k) \rightarrow (-(y_1-y_k),x_1-x_k) $$

Add back $(x_k,y_k)$.

$$ (x_k,y_k)+(-(y_1-y_k),x_1-x_k)=(x_k-(y_1-y_k),y_k+x_1-x_k) $$

Thus $(x_1,y_1)$ rotated about $(x_k,y_k)$ is $(x_k-(y_1-y_k),y_k+x_1-x_k)$.

def dragon_curve(n, color1='orange', *args, **kwargs):
    
    P = np.array([
        [0,0],
        [0,1]
    ])
    
    LRP = np.array([
        [0,0]
        ])
    
    for i in range(n):
        lenp = len(P)
        PoR = P[lenp-1]
        LP = P[lenp-2::-1]
        LRP = PoR
        for k in LP:
            RP = k - PoR
            RP = np.array([
                -RP[1],RP[0]
            ])
            RP = RP + PoR
            LRP = np.vstack([LRP,RP])
        P = np.vstack([P,LRP])
        
    plt.plot(P[:,0] ,P[:,1], *args, **kwargs)
    plt.plot(LRP[:,0] ,LRP[:,1],color=color1 , *args)
    
    ax = plt.gca()
    ax.axis('equal')
    
    plt.title("Dragon Curve Iteration #%d" % (n))
