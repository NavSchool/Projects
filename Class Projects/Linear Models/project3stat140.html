The Dataset: Heart Failure Clinical Records

The data set is a record of patients who had heart failure. Using the age of the patient, whether or not they have high blood pressure, checking their serum creatinine and serum sodium level, and ejection fraction percentage, we will try to predict whether the patient will pass away in the follow up period.

Conclusions

As of now the final model is okay at predicting whether the patient will pass away during the follow up period after a heart failure. With a 74% in both specificity and sensitivity, the model is good at predicting both if a patient will pass away and will survive. However, it it ideal to decrease the 25% misclassification rate since the misclassification 1 in 4 people is too much when the life of a person is in play. Future models should test the other variables to further increase the specificity and sensitivity and decrease the misclassification rate.

Interestingly, the fourth model (mod4) has a higher specificity and lower misclassification rate at the cost of a lower sensitivity.

It brings up the question whether a model with a higher sensitivity with a lower misclassification rate and specificity would be used over the final model. If it would be ever used in the real world, would it not be better to over catch people who could potentially die than under catch. With that said, it would be better to determined after a better future model is made.
